### 2.3 Part-Load Efficiency (IPLV/EER Ratings)

#### IPLV Calculation Method (ARI Standard)

**IPLV Formula**:
```
IPLV = 1 ÷ [(0.01/A) + (0.42/B) + (0.45/C) + (0.12/D)]

Where:
A = Efficiency at 100% load (EER or COP)
B = Efficiency at 75% load
C = Efficiency at 50% load
D = Efficiency at 25% load
```

**Weighting Factors Reflect Real Operation**:
- 100% load: 1% weighting (0.01)
- 75% load: 12% weighting (0.12)
- 50% load: 45% weighting (0.45)
- 25% load: 42% weighting (0.42)

> "Studies conducted by AHRI show that chillers run at full load only 1% of the time. When designing any chiller plant, part load efficiency must be taken into consideration since 99% of the operating hours for any chiller are on part load conditions."

#### Magnetic Bearing Part-Load Superiority

## 3. N+1 Redundancy Systems

### 3.1 Load Distribution Strategies for 3-Chiller Configuration

#### Real-World N+1 Example

**Oregon State University District Utility Plant**:
- **Configuration**: 3 × 1,500-ton magnetic-bearing centrifugal chillers
- **Total Capacity**: 4,500 tons
- **Redundancy**: N+1 (2,500-ton base + 1,500-ton redundancy = 150% of design load)
- **Cooling Towers**: 6 × 727-ton variable speed fans

**Design Philosophy**:
> "Multiple compressors are installed on a shared evaporator and condenser, providing redundancy to ensure efficient operation of the chiller."

#### Load Distribution Control Strategies

**Strategy 1: Sequential Loading** (Traditional)
- First chiller operates at highest PLR (Part Load Ratio)
- Second chiller stages on when first reaches capacity
- Third chiller provides backup/peak capacity

**Characteristics**:
- Maximizes efficiency of first unit
- Reduces cycling on backup units
- Simple control logic
- May not be optimal for magnetic bearing efficiency

**Strategy 2: Uniform Loading** (Balanced)
- All online chillers carry equal load
- Distributes wear evenly across equipment
- Enables maintenance rotation

**Characteristics**:
- Each chiller operates at same PLR
- Better for magnetic bearings (keeps all in efficiency sweet spot)
- More complex control
- Maximizes total plant efficiency

**Strategy 3: Advanced Load-Based Control** (Optimal)
> "Load-based staging strategy, where the load (tons cooling) on the four centrifugal chillers was monitored. Initial Stage On and Stage Off thresholds for each step were discussed, and we selected ones we believed would get us close to optimized energy performance, but not result in unnecessary cycling between stages."

**Implementation Example**:
```
Cooling Load < 800 tons: 1 chiller @ 50-90%
800-1600 tons: 2 chillers @ 40-80% each
1600-2500 tons: 2 chillers @ 80-100% + 1 chiller @ 0-60%
>2500 tons: 3 chillers @ balanced loads
```

### 3.2 Efficiency: Operating 1 of 3, 2 of 3, or 3 of 3 Units

#### Plant Efficiency Analysis (3 × 1,500-ton example)

**Configuration 1: Single Chiller Operation (1 of 3)**
- **Load Range**: 225-1,500 tons (15-100% of single unit)
- **Optimal Zone**: 750-1,275 tons (50-85% PLR single unit)
- **COP Range**: 8-10 (magnetic bearing sweet spot)
- **When to Use**: Building loads <1,200 tons

**Efficiency Characteristics**:
- Highest COP per chiller
- Lowest absolute plant kW consumption
- Best efficiency for low campus loads
- Risk: No redundancy during operation

**Configuration 2: Two Chiller Operation (2 of 3)**
- **Load Range**: 450-3,000 tons (15-100% per unit)
- **Optimal Zone**: 1,500-2,550 tons (50-85% PLR each)
- **COP Range**: 7-9 (still excellent efficiency)
- **When to Use**: Building loads 1,200-2,800 tons

**Efficiency Characteristics**:
- N+1 redundancy maintained (one backup available)
- Each chiller operates in high-efficiency zone
- Lower individual PLR = higher individual COP
- Preferred operating mode for most conditions

**Configuration 3: Three Chiller Operation (3 of 3)**
- **Load Range**: 675-4,500 tons (15-100% per unit)
- **Optimal Zone**: 2,250-3,825 tons (50-85% PLR each)
- **COP Range**: 6-8 (reduced but acceptable)
- **When to Use**: Building loads >2,800 tons or peak summer

**Efficiency Characteristics**:
- Peak capacity with no redundancy
- Highest plant kW consumption
- Individual units at lower PLR = better individual COP
- Use only when necessary for capacity

#### Series Configuration Advantage

**Advanced Strategy for Magnetic Bearing Systems**:
> "The chillers operated most efficiently at low load and low lift, which meant the overall control strategy needed to run as many chillers as stably possible to lower the load on each chiller, and the series configuration allowed for a reduction in lift seen by each chiller."

**Series Staging Benefits**:
- Reduces lift (temperature difference) for each chiller
- Enables stable operation of more chillers at lower individual loads
- Maximizes total plant efficiency
- Particularly effective for magnetic bearing technology

### 3.3 Optimal Staging Sequences for Part-Load Operation

#### Recommended Staging Sequence (3-Chiller Plant)

**Phase 1: Light Load (0-40% plant capacity)**
- **Active**: 1 chiller
- **PLR**: 15-60% (single unit)
- **Target**: Keep unit between 30-60% for efficiency
- **Action**: Stage on second chiller if approaching 70%

**Phase 2: Moderate Load (40-70% plant capacity)**
- **Active**: 2 chillers
- **PLR**: 30-50% each (balanced loading)
- **Target**: Maintain each unit 40-75% PLR
- **Action**: Stage third chiller if either unit exceeds 80%

**Phase 3: High Load (70-90% plant capacity)**
- **Active**: 2-3 chillers (transition zone)
- **PLR**: Depends on total load
- **Decision Logic**:
  - If 2 chillers sufficient at <85% each: Keep 2 online
  - If 2 chillers exceeding 85%: Bring online third chiller
- **Target**: Distribute load for optimal total plant efficiency

**Phase 4: Peak Load (>90% plant capacity)**
- **Active**: 3 chillers
- **PLR**: 50-85% each (balanced)
- **Target**: Share load equally
- **Alert**: Operating at or near design capacity (consider load shedding)

#### Anti-Short-Cycling Protection

**Minimum Runtime Logic**:
- Chiller must run ≥15 minutes before staging off
- Prevents excessive start/stop cycles
- Protects compressor electronics and magnetic bearings

**Staging Hysteresis**:
- Stage ON threshold: 80% PLR sustained for 5 minutes
- Stage OFF threshold: <50% PLR sustained for 10 minutes
- Dead band prevents hunting between stages

### 3.4 Energy Implications of Redundancy

#### Energy Cost Analysis

**Annual Energy Impact Example** (3 × 1,500-ton plant):

**Scenario A: N+1 Design (3 chillers for 3,000-ton load)**
- **Average Operating Mode**: 2 of 3 online
- **Average PLR per Chiller**: 50-60%
- **Plant COP**: 8-9
- **Annual Energy**: Baseline

**Scenario B: N Design (2 chillers for 3,000-ton load)**
- **Average Operating Mode**: 2 of 2 online
- **Average PLR per Chiller**: 75-90%
- **Plant COP**: 6-7 (reduced part-load operation)
- **Annual Energy**: +15-20% vs N+1
- **Risk**: No redundancy, higher failure risk

**Redundancy Energy Trade-off**:
- N+1 redundancy enables better part-load operation
- Individual chillers run at higher efficiency zones
- Paradoxically, having extra capacity REDUCES annual energy when properly controlled
- Magnetic bearing efficiency advantage compounds with proper staging

#### Documented Savings from Proper Staging

**OSU District Utility Plant Results**:
- **Savings**: >2 MWh electricity annually
- **Utility Incentives**: $420,000 in efficiency incentives
- **Configuration**: 3 × 1,500-ton magnetic-bearing chillers with intelligent staging

**Optimization Results from Field Studies**:
> "Particle Swarm Optimization search algorithm was employed to find optimum value of control parameters for the chiller plant with non-identical chillers. Holistic optimization achieves maximum savings: 10.5% of total chiller plant energy consumption on a hot day and 13.6% of total chiller plant energy consumption on a moderate day."

**Optimization Variables**:
1. Chilled water temperature setpoint
2. Condenser water temperature setpoint
3. Chiller stage sequence

**Key Insight**:
Proper staging control and redundancy design are critical to realizing magnetic bearing efficiency potential. The combination of:
- Right-sized equipment
- N+1 redundancy
- Intelligent load-based staging
- Magnetic bearing technology

Can achieve 10-50% energy savings compared to conventional systems with poor controls.

3. Adjust or replace TXV for R513A operating pressures
4. Charge with R513A (5.9% less charge mass)
5. Verify performance and adjust as needed

**Expected Results**:
- 56% GWP reduction (1,430 → 631)
- 3.5% energy savings
- No safety class change (remains A1)

**Challenges**:
- 22% capacity reduction requires compensation

**When to Consider**:
- Future-proofing new installations
- Existing system oversized by 20%+
- Regulatory requirements demand ultra-low GWP
- Willing to accept capacity trade-off for efficiency gain

**System Modifications**:
1. Assess capacity shortfall (22% reduction)
2. Evaluate options: Add chiller capacity, accept lower output, or enhance heat exchangers
3. Implement A2L safety measures (if required by code):
   - Refrigerant sensors and alarms
   - Ventilation modifications
   - Equipment room classification review
4. Update controls for different operating pressures
5. Complete refrigerant changeover and commissioning

#### R1234yf Retrofit (Moderate Complexity)

**Considerations**:
- Better GWP than R513A (4 vs 631)
- Moderate performance penalty (5-9% COP reduction)
- A2L classification requires safety assessment
- Automotive industry volume driving cost down

- Safety compliance requirements
- Acceptable performance trade-off
